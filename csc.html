Course Description

Computer Science is the study of computation, information, and automation. This course dives deep into the mathematical foundations of algorithms, the complexity of data structures, and the architecture of modern operating systems. Students will explore how hardware and software interact to solve complex problems, ranging from Big Data analysis to the development of intelligent autonomous systems.

Learning Objectives

  • Master Big-O notation and algorithmic complexity analysis.
  • Implement advanced data structures including Trees, Graphs, and Hash Tables.
  • Understand the principles of Automata Theory and Formal Languages.
  • Design and optimize relational and non-relational database systems.
  • Explore the Von Neumann architecture and CPU instruction sets.
  • Apply Discrete Mathematics to solve computational logic problems.
  • Develop multi-threaded applications and understand concurrency.
  • Understand the layers of the TCP/IP networking stack.
  • Evaluate Machine Learning models and Artificial Neural Networks.
  • Practice ethical computing and data privacy in the digital age.
