Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the fundamental principles of cybersecurity. Students will explore the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability), risk management, and the evolving threat landscape. The curriculum covers defense-in-depth strategies, including network security, cryptography, ethical hacking, and incident response, preparing students to defend digital assets against sophisticated global adversaries.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the core principles of Information Assurance (CIA Triad).
  • Identify and analyze common cyber threats, including malware, phishing, and DDoS.
  • Implement cryptographic solutions to protect data at rest and in transit.
  • Perform vulnerability assessments and basic penetration testing.
  • Configure firewalls, Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), and VPNs.
  • Apply identity and access management (IAM) best practices.
  • Develop incident response and disaster recovery plans.
  • Understand the legal, ethical, and regulatory aspects of cybersecurity (GDPR, HIPAA).
  • Evaluate and mitigate risks within a corporate IT infrastructure.
  • Foster a security-first mindset in system administration and development.
