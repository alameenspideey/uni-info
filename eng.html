Mechatronic Engineering

Engineering the Future: Integrating Mechanics, Electronics, and Intelligent Control

12 Weeks
Professional Level
4 Credits
Spring 2026

Course Description

Mechatronic Engineering is a multidisciplinary field that bridges the gap between mechanical engineering and electronics. This course focuses on the design, construction, and operation of automated systems and robotics. You will explore how to integrate sensors, actuators, and microcontrollers to create intelligent machines capable of interacting with their physical environment.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the synergy between mechanical, electrical, and computer systems.
  • Design and simulate 3D mechanical components using CAD software.
  • Master circuit analysis and the implementation of analog/digital electronics.
  • Program microcontrollers (Arduino, STM32) for real-time machine control.
  • Apply Control Theory to manage system stability and feedback loops.
  • Select and interface sensors (Lidar, Ultrasonic, IR) and actuators (Servos, Steppers).
  • Develop PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) programs for industrial automation.
  • Analyze robotic kinematics and dynamics for path planning.
  • Implement Signal Processing techniques for noisy sensor data.
  • Understand the principles of Industry 4.0 and Internet of Things (IoT).
