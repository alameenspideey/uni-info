Food Science & Technology

Innovating Global Food Systems through Science and Safety

12 Weeks
Undergraduate Level
4 Credits
Spring 2026

Course Description

This course explores the physical, biological, and chemical makeup of food, and the concepts underlying food processing. You will learn the scientific principles of food preservation, the development of new food products, and the rigorous quality control standards required to maintain public health. From farm to fork, we examine how technology ensures the sustainability and safety of our global food supply.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the chemical composition of foods and how processing affects nutrition.
  • Identify food-borne pathogens and apply microbiological safety principles.
  • Analyze food preservation techniques such as thermal processing, freezing, and dehydration.
  • Design food products that meet consumer sensory and nutritional demands.
  • Apply HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point) systems for food safety.
  • Evaluate food packaging materials and their impact on shelf life.
  • Navigate international food laws and labeling regulations.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in laboratory techniques for food analysis.
  • Understand the principles of sustainable food manufacturing.
  • Develop a professional commitment to food quality and public safety.
