Course Description

Medical Laboratory Science (MLS) is a critical healthcare field that combines biology and chemistry with advanced technology to analyze biological specimens. In this course, students learn to perform complex tests on blood, tissues, and other body fluids. You will gain the skills necessary to provide physicians with the accurate data needed to diagnose, treat, and prevent diseases such as cancer, diabetes, and infectious illnesses.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the principles of clinical chemistry and metabolic analysis.
  • Master hematology and the identification of cellular blood disorders.
  • Perform microbiological cultures and sensitivity testing for infectious diseases.
  • Apply immunology and blood banking (immunohematology) procedures.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in urinalysis and body fluid analysis.
  • Ensure laboratory safety and strictly follow Biohazard protocols.
  • Implement Quality Control (QC) and Laboratory Information Systems (LIS).
  • Interpret diagnostic data to assist in clinical decision-making.
  • Understand the ethical and legal standards of patient confidentiality (HIPAA).
  • Maintain and calibrate advanced automated diagnostic machinery.
