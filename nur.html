Course Description

The Nursing Science course is designed to equip students with the knowledge and clinical skills required to provide high-quality healthcare. You will study human anatomy, physiology, and pharmacology, while developing the critical thinking skills needed for patient assessment and emergency intervention. The curriculum emphasizes evidence-based practice, ethical care, and the promotion of health across diverse populations.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply the Nursing Process (Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation).
  • Master clinical skills including wound care, IV administration, and vital sign monitoring.
  • Understand pharmacology and the safe administration of medications.
  • Develop advanced patient communication and therapeutic relationship skills.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in maternal and child health nursing.
  • Ensure patient safety through strict adherence to infection control protocols.
  • Analyze ethical and legal issues in healthcare delivery.
  • Perform physical assessments across the lifespan.
  • Understand the pathophysiology of common acute and chronic illnesses.
  • Collaborate effectively within multidisciplinary healthcare teams.
