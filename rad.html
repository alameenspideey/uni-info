Course Description

Radiography is a dynamic healthcare profession that uses ionizing and non-ionizing radiation to produce images of the internal structures of the human body. This course covers the fundamental principles of radiographic positioning, radiation physics, and digital image processing. Students will learn to operate high-tech imaging equipment while ensuring patient safety through strict adherence to radiation protection protocols.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the physics of X-ray production and interaction with matter.
  • Master radiographic positioning for skeletal and soft tissue imaging.
  • Apply ALARA (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) principles for radiation safety.
  • Identify anatomical structures and pathological conditions on medical images.
  • Operate digital radiography (DR) and computed radiography (CR) systems.
  • Learn the basics of advanced modalities: CT, MRI, and Ultrasound.
  • Practice professional patient care and communication in a clinical setting.
  • Evaluate image quality and perform radiographic critique.
  • Understand the ethical and legal aspects of medical imaging and records.
  • Implement contrast media administration and emergency protocols.
